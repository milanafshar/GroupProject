Northwestern Students React to Sarah Jessica Parker as 2026 Commencement Speaker

Sarah Jessica Parker announced as Northwestern University's 2026 commencement speaker. Credit: Wikimedia Commons

Northwestern University made waves on campus this week with the announcement that actress and producer Sarah Jessica Parker, best known for her iconic role as Carrie Bradshaw in Sex and the City will deliver the keynote address at the Class of 2026 commencement ceremony. The news spread quickly across social media, sparking a wave of reactions ranging from excitement to surprise.

For many seniors, the announcement came as an unexpected but welcome surprise. Parker, who has also built a career as an author advocate and entrepreneur, brings a different profile than the political figures and tech executives who have historically graced Northwestern's commencement stage.

Reactions on campus were immediate and split. Some students couldn't hide their enthusiasm, while others were left questioning the choice.

One senior in the School of Communication said she was completely caught off guard when she saw the announcement pop up on her phone. ***Click play to hear her reaction****

Not everyone was immediately sold on the pick. A Weinberg junior studying political science raised an eyebrow at the announcement, wondering aloud whether Parker's body of work translates to the kind of gravitas typically expected of a commencement speaker. ***Click play to hear her reaction****

Still, others pointed out that commencement speakers don't have to be world leaders to deliver a meaningful message. Parker's career trajectory — from a working-class upbringing in Ohio to global pop culture icon — is itself a story of resilience and reinvention that could resonate deeply with graduating seniors stepping into an uncertain world.

A Medill senior said Parker's personal story was exactly what she wanted to hear as she prepares to graduate into a turbulent media industry. ***Click play to hear her reaction****

Faculty members also weighed in. Some professors noted that choosing a cultural figure over a traditional speaker signals a broader shift in how universities are thinking about commencement — less as a formal institutional ritual and more as a celebration that meets students where they are.

One Medill faculty member, who has attended dozens of commencement ceremonies over the years, said the choice reflects a generational shift in what students want from these milestone moments. ***Click play to hear her reaction****

Of course, no campus announcement is complete without at least one student channeling their inner Carrie Bradshaw. One senior joked that she had been waiting her whole college career for a speaker who truly understood the existential weight of choosing the right pair of shoes. ***Click play to hear her reaction****

Whatever one's take on the pick, the announcement has done something that few commencement speaker reveals manage to do: get people talking. As the Class of 2026 prepares for its final chapter at Northwestern, Sarah Jessica Parker's address is shaping up to be one of the most anticipated in recent memory — whether students are tuning in for the fashion icon, the storyteller, or simply out of curiosity.

The 2026 Northwestern Commencement Ceremony is scheduled for June 13th, 2026 at the United Center in Chicago.
